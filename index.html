Project 2: Responsive Redesign

Introduction

For this project, I found a website that was poorly designed and created a responsively redesigned website to address some of the problems I identified using Bootstrap's CSS components library. I also practiced low-fi prototyping with Balsamiq and hi-fi prototyping with Figma.

Part 1: Identifying Usability Problems

I chose to redesign a registration page for an event at a local magnet school. I chose this site because I think it is very text-dense and difficult to know what to do or how to proceed with registering (even though this would presumably be the main task for anyone arriving at this page).

Original Website

Usability

Looking at the website, I first tried to find usability problems with the interface. Although not being visually pleasing doesn't always translate into usability problems, this website suffers from both a lack of aesthetics and usability problems.

I identified the following usability problems with the interface:

  • Text sizing is arbitrary, with important information such as headings not being significantly bigger than other text.
  • A table is used to lay out information, which is unnecessary, visually confusing, and ugly. The table also does not resize responsively.
  • The home button takes you to the homepage, while the logo button (which by convention also takes you home) justs navigates to the same page.
  • The word "register" and its forms is used to refer both to registration for this specific event and registration for an account on the webiste.
  • Information about the event is given redundantly.
  • Users are not told whether they are logged in or not, an are instead asked to recall whether or not they have previously made an account. However, information pertaining to event registration (which cannot proceed unless logged in) is present before users even log in.
  • Irrelevant information (random numbers) appears in the middle of the page, with no indication what the numbers indicate or whether or not they are important.
  • If selecting "Yes" or "No" to indicate that an account has been made already, no option is given to go back if the wrong selection is made.
  • There are four links to various social media sites under the "Connect with us" in the footer, yet none of them render since there is no link content.

Accessibility

I then used the WAVE accessibility tool to look at the accessibility of the website. Most of the findings in the report were in line with what I had found to be usability issues (i.e. empty links, table layout), which demonstrates that accessibiliity and good design often go hand-in-hand. Many of the other issues, such as mising page regions, alt text, or underlined text, could have been fixed by following more proper HTML conventions.
